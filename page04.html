I2VGen-XL: High-Quality Image-to-Video Synthesis
Via Cascaded Diffusion Models

Shiwei Zhang*, Jiayu Wang*, Yingya Zhang*, Kang Zhao, Hangjie Yuan,
Zhiwu Qing, Xiang Wang, Deli Zhao, Jingren Zhou

Alibaba Group

Video synthesis has recently made remarkable strides benefiting from the rapid development of diffusion models. However, it sill encounter challenges in terms of semantic accuracy, clarity and spatio-temporal continuity. They are primarily raise from the scarcity of well-aligned video-text data and the complex inherent structure of videos, making it difficult for models to simultaneously ensure both semantic and qualitative excellence. In this work, we propose a cascaded I2VGen-XL approach that enhances model performance by decoupling these two factors and ensures the alignment of the input data by utilizing static images as crucial guidance. I2VGen-XL consists of two stages: i) the base stage guarantees coherent semantic and preserves content from input images by utilizing two hierarchical encoders, and ii) the refinement stage enhances the video's details, ensuring its consistency and improving the resolution (1280x720), by incorporating an additional brief text. To improve the diversity, we collect around 35 million single-shot text-videos and 6 billion text-images and conduct joint image-video training when optimizing the model. Thus I2VGen-XL is capable of simultaneously enhancing the semantic accuracy, continuity of details and clarity of the generated videos. Extensive experiments have investigated the principles underlying I2VGen-XL and compared it with the current state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating its effectiveness on diverse data. The source code and models will be publicly available.
